While exercising, there is no “one fits all” approach. Every individual reacts differently to various forms of feedback, highlighting the importance of tailoring exercise programs to meet each person’s unique needs and preferences. My research focuses on this personalized approach by utilizing a conversational robot. The robot will engage the  users from the first interaction, carefully analyzing their attitudes towards exercising, their expressed motivations, and their mental states. 

Initial conversations can provide invaluable insights, allowing exercise coaches and physical therapists  to gauge a person's motivation level and tailor the coaching style right from the start. This adaptive interaction helps to craft a personalized feedback style  that are not only effective but also enjoyable, fostering a positive and sustainable relationship with physical activity. By integrating user-specific preferences, this project aims to motivate aging adults do exercise.

This work is being done under the supervision of [Dr. Aaron Steinfeld](https://www.cs.cmu.edu/~astein/), in collaboration with [Roshni Kaushik](https://roshk99.github.io/index.html) and [Dr. Reid Simmons](https://www.cs.cmu.edu/~reids/), at Carnegie Mellon University.

All work is funded through the [AI-CARING](https://ai-caring.org/) project.
